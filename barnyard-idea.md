# Barnyard

Barnyard is a background authenticity layer for intimate photos.

The product is not "proof on the blockchain" or "AI detection." The product is this:

Someone sends you a photo of themselves, and if both sides are using Barnyard, the photo gets through because it was captured as a real moment and sealed at the moment of capture. If it was generated, replayed, or altered, Barnyard can decide later that it was never real.

## The Core Move

Replace "say cheese" with a secret phrase that changes every second.

Not a public clock. A private Barnyard clock.

At `09:47:23`, Barnyard might know that the phrase is `velvet architect`.
At `09:47:24`, it might be `broken lighthouse`.

Only Barnyard knows the mapping.

When a user takes a selfie, the app briefly prompts the current phrase and captures it in the Live Photo or short motion/audio window that phones already record. The user is doing something normal: taking a photo and saying a phrase out loud. The recipient is doing nothing special at all.

## User Experience

### Sender

1. Open camera.
2. Take a selfie.
3. Barnyard flashes a phrase for that second.
4. User says the phrase instead of "cheese."
5. Photo is sent normally.

### Receiver

1. A photo arrives by text or inside the Barnyard-aware messaging layer.
2. Barnyard silently checks whether the photo has a valid seal.
3. If yes, the image appears normally.
4. If no, it is held back, blurred, or marked unverified.

The ideal experience is that the user barely notices Barnyard until the day it saves them.

## What Gets Sealed

Barnyard's wax seal is four things together:

1. Face match: this is the enrolled person.
2. Capture-time phrase: the person said the secret phrase for that exact second.
3. Motion/audio congruence: the mouth movement, voice, and live image window agree.
4. Entanglement package: metadata and visual context that binds the image to its moment.

The phrase is the sharpest part of the seal because it is time-specific, private, and short enough for normal people to say naturally.

## Why The Secret Clock Matters

If the clock were public, an attacker could precompute or instantly synthesize the correct phrase.

Because the clock is private:

- The phrase is not knowable in advance.
- The phrase is not inferable from the final image alone.
- The phrase only exists inside Barnyard's trust boundary.

The sender sees it at capture time. The receiver never needs to.

Later, if needed, Barnyard can ask:

"At this claimed timestamp, what should the phrase have been?"

Then it checks the audio/video evidence against that answer.

## Why This Is Better Than Slapping Metadata On A Photo

Plain metadata is forgeable.
NFT-style provenance is external and dumb.
AI detectors rot.

Barnyard is stronger because the proof is partly inside the moment itself:

- the face
- the voice
- the spoken phrase
- the exact second
- the live-motion window

That means the attacker is not just forging a file. They are forging a synchronized event.

## Threat Model

Barnyard is built for this future:

- a friend's phone number can be flooded by generated junk
- you may receive many convincing fakes
- text channels may be compromised
- old real photos may be replayed
- synthetic audio/video may be good enough to fool humans

Barnyard is trying to defeat:

- replay attacks
- stale real media reused as current
- AI-generated selfies
- intercepted and altered images
- mass fake-image spam

Barnyard is not guaranteed to defeat:

- full device compromise on the sender's phone
- a perfect real-time model with Barnyard clock access and exact biometric voice/video control
- coercion in the real world

That is fine. The goal is not metaphysical certainty. The goal is to make faking a trusted intimate photo much harder than generating a generic fake.

## The Barnyard Test

For a photo to count as real, Barnyard should be able to say:

"This is Dave. He appears to be saying the phrase Barnyard assigned to that second. The Live Photo timing matches. The capture package is internally coherent. Nothing about this looks replayed or synthesized."

That is enough.

## Product Definition

Barnyard is:

- a camera layer
- a private phrase clock
- a sealing service
- a receiving filter

Barnyard is not:

- a social network
- an NFT project
- a blockchain company
- a generic deepfake detector
- a public timestamp registry

## The Secret Phrase Clock

The phrase clock should have these properties:

- two-word phrases
- easy to say
- easy to hear
- low collision rate
- emotionally neutral or vivid, but not embarrassing

Examples:

- `velvet architect`
- `broken lighthouse`
- `cerulean mandolin`
- `hidden terrace`
- `mild volcano`

It should change exactly on second boundaries and be generated by a secret seed that Barnyard never exposes publicly.

## MVP

The first version does not need to solve every problem.

It only needs to do this:

1. Show a phrase at capture time.
2. Record short motion plus audio.
3. Package the seal.
4. Upload a dense capture manifest to Barnyard.
5. Let a receiving Barnyard client decide whether to admit the image.

### MVP inputs

- selfie image
- live-motion clip
- audio snippet
- timestamp
- device attestation if available
- optional coarse location

### MVP server checks

- face matches enrolled user
- spoken phrase matches expected phrase for timestamp
- lip movement roughly aligns
- capture package hash matches what was uploaded

### MVP receiver behavior

- verified: show image normally
- uncertain: show warning
- failed: quarantine

## What Makes It Good

The strongest thing about Barnyard is not the crypto. It is that it quietly converts a fakeability problem into a coordination problem that the attacker has to solve in real time.

An attacker now has to synthesize:

- the right person
- the right phrase
- the right second
- the right voice
- the right mouth movement
- the right capture coherence

That is much harder than just making "a photo of Dave."

## Good Language

How to talk about it:

- "Proof you're real."
- "Photos that arrive with a seal."
- "Say the phrase. Send the photo."
- "Barnyard lets real photos through."

How not to talk about it:

- "cryptographic provenance"
- "blockchain-backed identity"
- "NFT for selfies"
- "decentralized trust layer"

Those are all poison.

## What The Company Is Selling

Barnyard sells one thing:

Confidence that an intimate photo was captured as a real event and not synthesized later.

Not certainty forever.
Not metaphysics.
Just confidence strong enough to be useful when the channel is poisoned.

## The Sharpest Version

The complete idea, in one sentence:

Barnyard is a camera and messaging layer that uses a secret second-by-second spoken phrase clock plus live capture evidence to seal selfies at the moment they are taken, so fake or replayed intimate photos can be silently filtered out later.
